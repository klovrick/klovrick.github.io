My name? Baby.

Life has been a wild ride for me so far. When I was younger, I lost both my parents in a car accident and developed tinnitus, leading to a constant ringing in my ears.

While my dad was an abusive alcoholic, my mom was the most caring person anyone could have ever known. She was a phenomenal singer and whenever I’m reminded of her, I find myself missing her a lot. Since then, I play music to drown it all out - the ringing and the memories.

Fast forward to the present.

She's beautiful.

She tells me her name is Debora and that she just recently started waitressing at this diner. While she asks me for my order, I can only think of one thing that I want. Her. It’s inexplicable, but I have an uncontainable desire get to know her.

She explains to me that one day, she dreams of being able to head West with just her, music, and the road.

Hearing her sing throughout the diner, I was instantly captivated.

I'm a driver.

Like a chauffeur, my job is to drive around important people. I would describe it as exciting, thrilling even. I rarely work with the same people and don’t have regular hours, so it’s not really a normal job. When they call me, I go. I don’t have a choice.

Doc organizes our routes and my job is to follow his plans exactly. No questions asked.

it's not what I want

I said my job is exciting. But it’s also dangerous. And hell, it’s even deadly. We partner with illegal weapons dealers, we rob businesses, and sometimes, we kill.

I don’t want to be part of this life anymore. I can’t stand putting people in danger. And worse, I can’t deal with the fact that I may be starting to enjoy the rush.
